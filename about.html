About Chris

I have been brewing for about 15 years starting with a few extract kits and getting into all grain after that. I am a BJCP judge and have also worked in the Craft Beer business for Offshore Ales. My passion in brewing is mainly around Belgian and English inspired Ales.


The Binche Brewing Project

I have been keeping track of my beers over time via recipe journals but wanted to create this online journal and project of sorts to track progress/my thoughts as a brewer and a homebrewery. This visual journal will take the place of the analog journal of recipes I've been keeping.

What's up with the Binche name? Belgium and brewing in the Belgian tradition has always been a huge influence on how I approach beer and homebrewing. Belgium has a diverse range of beer "styles" and breweries from old school to new school. Belgian brewers tend to throw out the rules and focus on flavors rather than a strict historical approach..

Binche is a Walloon city and municipality located in the Belgian province of Hainaut, the origin of my favorite style of beer in the world; Saison. So as a homage to my favorite style, I named this brewing project after the area.

The Farmhouse

Me, my wife, our two rdogs and six chickens live on about an acre of land of which 1/4 of that is set aside for organic food production including hops and grains (a project I'm currently working on). So I guess I can finally call myself a Farmhouse Brewer in the modern sense of farming. We are on Cape Cod in Massachusetts surrounded by the Atlantic. This is where I brew and this property is a huge part of our lives.


The Setup

I brew everything from 10 gallon batches on a propane burner all the way down to 2 gallon batches on our electric stove. I brew all grain using a method called Brew In A Bag (BIAB) but I use a stainless steel basket. I ferment most of my beers in a stainless 7 gallon fermentor and keg or bottle the final product depending on carbonation levels I'm looking for.
